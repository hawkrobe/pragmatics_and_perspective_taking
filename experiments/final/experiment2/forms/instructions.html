Guess My Object!

In this experiment you will play a fun communication game with another turker! One of you will be assigned the Speaker role and the other will be the Listener. On each round of the game, we will randomly pick a few simple objects like these:

and place them in a grid of cubby-holes between you. Although you will just see a 2D view in the game, you can imagine you are sitting across the table from your partner:

One of the objects in the grid is the target, which is outlined in orange. Only the Speaker can see which object is the target:

The goal of the Speaker is to send a message so that the Listener can figure out which object is the target. You'll both get a bonus of $0.01 for each correct response, so pay attention!

The goal of the Listener is to identify the target based on the message sent by the Speaker. If you are the Listener, you will first need to wait until the Speaker has sent their message before being able to click on the object you think they're telling you about or replying through the chatbox.

Once the Listener clicks on the object they believe is the target, both players will be given feedback: the Speaker will see what the Listener clicked, and the Listener will see the true target. Then you will both automatically advance to the next round of objects. There are a total of 24 rounds. After the final round you will answer a few more questions and be on your way.

Hidden objects

What makes this game more interesting is that on some rounds, curtains will appear in some cells. These curtains block the view of the Speaker, who cannot know what object, if any, is behind the curtain. To illustrate, here is the Speaker's view and Listener's view of the same grid, side by side. The Listener can see that there is a green, checkered circle on their side of the curtain, but the Speaker does not know it is there.

A few final notes: First, since you are playing with another player, you may see this screen before the game begins:

Just hold tight. Another player should join the game within the next few minutes. If you're in the waiting room 15 minutes without another player joining, please return the HIT and try again later!

Second, please be respectful of the worker you're playing with: pay close attention and try to be as responsive as possible. During each round, please talk only about the game and please use properties of the object instead of simply describing locations in the grid. We will be recording the messages you send for research purposes.

Finally, please DO NOT refresh the page at any point once you start the game (including the waiting page). If you refresh, you will not be able to rejoin the game you were in and you may not be able to submit the HIT.
